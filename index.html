How it works

1) Feed

Organic waste enters via the substrate inlet.

2) Digest

Anaerobic microbes produce biogas in the digester.

3) Store

Biogas is stored in an airtight reservoir.

4) Use

Gas powers a burner or lighting appliance.

5) Return

Digestate exits as a liquid biofertilizer.

1 kg of kitchen waste can generate up to ~0.45 m³ of biogas (typical average from trials; actual yield depends on feedstock & conditions).

Frequently Asked Questions

What waste can I feed into the digester?

Typical kitchen scraps, fruit/vegetable waste, and suitable farm residues. Avoid plastics, metals, and chemicals.

How much gas can it produce?

Yields vary; as a guide, ~1 kg of kitchen waste can produce up to ~0.45 m³ of biogas under favorable conditions.

Does it work without grid power?

Yes — the mixing system is solar‑powered and the process itself is anaerobic.

Who is it for?

Households, restaurants, markets, schools, hospitals, and farms seeking onsite waste-to-energy.
